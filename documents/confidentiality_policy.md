# **Annexure C**

## KISAAN INFOTECH LIMITED

### **CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION POLICY**

---

## **1. POLICY OVERVIEW**

### **1.1 Purpose**
This Confidentiality and Data Protection Policy establishes guidelines for handling, protecting, and maintaining the confidentiality of sensitive information belonging to Kisaan Infotech Limited ("the Company"), its customers, farmers, business partners, and employees. This policy aims to:
- Protect the Company's proprietary and confidential information
- Ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws in India
- Maintain trust and integrity in business relationships
- Prevent unauthorized disclosure, misuse, or loss of confidential data
- Define employee responsibilities regarding information security

### **1.2 Scope**
This policy applies to:
- All employees (permanent, probationary, contractual, and temporary)
- Consultants, contractors, and third-party service providers
- Interns, trainees, and apprentices
- All forms of information (physical, electronic, verbal, and visual)
- Information accessed, created, stored, or transmitted during employment

### **1.3 Effective Date**
This policy is effective from the date of employee's joining and continues to remain in force even after termination of employment.

### **1.4 Policy Owner**
This policy is owned and maintained by the Management of Kisaan Infotech Limited. Any questions or clarifications should be directed to the HR Department or immediate supervisor.

## **2. DEFINITIONS**

### **2.1 Confidential Information**
Confidential Information includes, but is not limited to:

**A. Business Information**
- Business strategies, plans, and forecasts
- Marketing and sales strategies
- Pricing information, cost structures, and profit margins
- Financial data, budgets, and projections
- Procurement and sourcing strategies
- Vendor and supplier information
- Business proposals and contracts
- Market research and competitive intelligence
- Operational procedures and processes
- Trade secrets and proprietary methodologies

**B. Farmer & Customer Data**
- Farmer personal details (names, addresses, contact numbers, bank details, Aadhaar, PAN)
- Farmer production data, crop patterns, and landholding information
- Procurement prices, payment terms, and transaction history
- Customer details (names, addresses, contact information)
- Customer orders, purchase history, and preferences
- Delivery addresses and schedules
- Credit terms and payment information
- Customer complaints and feedback

**C. Employee Information**
- Personal employee data (addresses, contact details, family information)
- Salary information and compensation details
- Performance reviews and appraisal records
- Disciplinary actions and grievances
- Medical records and health information
- Bank account details and tax information

**D. Technical & Operational Information**
- Product specifications and quality standards
- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
- Quality control processes and testing methods
- Packaging and branding specifications
- Logistics routes and distribution networks
- Storage and warehousing details
- Software systems, databases, and passwords
- Technical documentation and manuals

**E. Legal & Compliance Information**
- Contracts and agreements
- Legal disputes and litigation matters
- Regulatory compliance reports
- Audit reports and findings
- Licenses and certifications
- Insurance policies and claims

**F. Intellectual Property**
- Innovations, inventions, and improvements
- Brand names, logos, and trademarks
- Copyrighted materials
- Designs and prototypes
- Research and development work
- Domain names and digital assets

### **2.2 Personal Data**
Personal Data means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including:
- Biographical data (name, age, gender, date of birth)
- Contact information (address, phone number, email)
- Identification numbers (Aadhaar, PAN, driving license)
- Financial information (bank accounts, payment details)
- Biometric data (fingerprints, facial recognition)
- Photographic images
- Any other information that can identify an individual

### **2.3 Sensitive Personal Data**
Sensitive Personal Data includes:
- Passwords and financial information (bank account, credit card, debit card)
- Physical, physiological, and mental health conditions
- Medical records and history
- Biometric information
- Sexual orientation
- Any information received for processing, stored, or processed under lawful contract

### **2.4 Data Processing**
Any operation performed on data including collection, recording, storage, retrieval, use, disclosure, transmission, or destruction.

## **3. CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS**

### **3.1 General Obligations**
All employees must:

1. **Maintain Confidentiality:** Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential during and after employment

2. **Use Only for Business Purposes:** Use Confidential Information solely for performing authorized job duties

3. **Need-to-Know Basis:** Access only the information necessary for job responsibilities

4. **Prevent Unauthorized Disclosure:** Take all reasonable measures to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure

5. **Secure Storage:** Store documents and data securely when not in use

6. **Secure Communication:** Use approved channels and methods for sharing confidential information

7. **Report Breaches:** Immediately report any suspected or actual breach of confidentiality

8. **Return Materials:** Return all confidential materials upon termination or when no longer needed

### **3.2 Prohibited Actions**
Employees must NOT:

1. Disclose Confidential Information to unauthorized persons, including family members and friends

2. Discuss confidential matters in public places (restaurants, public transport, social gatherings)

3. Remove confidential documents from office premises without authorization

4. Copy, photograph, or reproduce confidential materials without permission

5. Use Confidential Information for personal benefit or gain

6. Use Confidential Information to compete with the Company

7. Disclose Confidential Information to competitors, media, or any third parties

8. Post or share confidential information on social media, forums, or public platforms

9. Access information beyond their authorized level or job requirements

10. Share passwords, login credentials, or access codes with others

## **4. DATA PROTECTION PRINCIPLES**

The Company and its employees shall adhere to the following data protection principles:

### **4.1 Lawfulness and Fairness**
- Data shall be collected and processed lawfully and fairly
- Purpose of data collection shall be clearly communicated
- Consent shall be obtained where required by law

### **4.2 Purpose Limitation**
- Data shall be collected only for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes
- Data shall not be used for purposes incompatible with the original purpose

### **4.3 Data Minimization**
- Only necessary and relevant data shall be collected
- Excessive or irrelevant data collection shall be avoided

### **4.4 Accuracy**
- Data shall be accurate, complete, and kept up to date
- Reasonable steps shall be taken to correct or delete inaccurate data

### **4.5 Storage Limitation**
- Data shall be retained only for as long as necessary for the stated purpose
- Data shall be securely deleted or destroyed when no longer required

### **4.6 Integrity and Confidentiality**
- Appropriate security measures shall be implemented to protect data
- Data shall be protected against unauthorized access, loss, damage, or destruction

### **4.7 Accountability**
- The Company shall be accountable for compliance with data protection principles
- Records of data processing activities shall be maintained

## **5. DATA SECURITY MEASURES**

### **5.1 Physical Security**

**Documents and Records:**
- Store all physical documents containing confidential information in locked cabinets/drawers
- Do not leave confidential documents unattended on desks
- Shred or destroy confidential documents before disposal
- Use secure disposal methods (cross-cut shredding, incineration)
- Lock office premises when leaving
- Do not carry confidential documents unnecessarily outside office

**Access Control:**
- Restrict access to sensitive areas (server rooms, file storage areas)
- Maintain visitor logs and escort visitors in restricted areas
- Use ID cards and access control systems where applicable
- Implement clean desk policy at end of workday

### **5.2 Digital Security**

**Computer and Device Security:**
- Use strong passwords (minimum 8 characters with alphanumeric and special characters)
- Change passwords regularly (every 90 days minimum)
- Do not share passwords or login credentials
- Lock computer/device when leaving workstation (Ctrl+Alt+Del or screen lock)
- Do not save passwords in browsers or unencrypted files
- Install and update antivirus software regularly
- Do not download unauthorized software or applications
- Encrypt sensitive data stored on laptops or portable devices

**Email Security:**
- Use company email only for business communication
- Do not send confidential information to personal email accounts
- Verify recipient email addresses before sending confidential information
- Mark emails containing sensitive information as "Confidential"
- Use encryption for highly sensitive email communications
- Do not open suspicious emails or attachments from unknown senders
- Report phishing attempts to IT department immediately

**Data Storage:**
- Store data only on authorized company servers or systems
- Do not store company data on personal devices without authorization
- Use approved cloud storage services only (if permitted)
- Regularly backup important data as per company procedures
- Delete temporary files and clear cache regularly

**Mobile Device Security:**
- Enable PIN/password protection on all mobile devices used for work
- Do not leave mobile devices unattended in public places
- Enable remote wipe capability for devices containing sensitive data
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately
- Do not connect to unsecured public Wi-Fi networks for accessing company data

**Network Security:**
- Use VPN (Virtual Private Network) when accessing company systems remotely
- Do not bypass firewall or security controls
- Do not connect unauthorized devices to company network
- Report suspicious network activity immediately

### **5.3 Communication Security**

**Verbal Communication:**
- Do not discuss confidential matters in public places or where unauthorized persons can overhear
- Use private meeting rooms for confidential discussions
- Close doors and windows during sensitive conversations
- Be mindful of phone conversations in open areas

**Written Communication:**
- Mark documents containing confidential information as "Confidential" or "Restricted"
- Use "Confidential - To Be Opened by Addressee Only" envelopes for sensitive documents
- Use registered post or courier with tracking for sending confidential documents
- Verify recipient details before sending communications

**Social Media:**
- Do not post or share any company information on social media platforms
- Do not discuss work-related matters on social networking sites
- Maintain professional discretion in online interactions
- Report any social media breach or data leak immediately

## **6. SPECIFIC DATA PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS**

### **6.1 Farmer Data Protection**

**Collection:**
- Collect farmer data only for legitimate business purposes (procurement, payment, communication)
- Obtain verbal or written consent from farmers for data collection
- Inform farmers about how their data will be used
- Collect only necessary information (name, contact, bank details, land details)

**Usage:**
- Use farmer data only for procurement, payment processing, and communication
- Do not use farmer data for unauthorized marketing or third-party sharing
- Maintain confidentiality of farmer pricing and transaction information
- Do not disclose one farmer's data to another farmer

**Storage:**
- Maintain farmer database in secure, password-protected systems
- Restrict access to farmer data to authorized personnel only
- Keep physical farmer records in locked storage
- Maintain separate records for each farmer with proper filing

**Protection:**
- Do not share farmer contact details with competitors or unauthorized parties
- Protect farmer bank account and payment information with highest security
- Do not use farmer data for personal benefit or unauthorized purposes
- Ensure farmers' Aadhaar and PAN information is stored securely and used only for statutory compliance

### **6.2 Customer Data Protection**

**Collection:**
- Collect customer data only for order processing, delivery, and customer service
- Obtain consent for data collection and usage
- Inform customers about data usage and retention policies

**Usage:**
- Use customer data only for fulfilling orders and providing services
- Do not use customer data for unauthorized marketing without consent
- Maintain confidentiality of customer purchase history and preferences

**Storage:**
- Store customer data securely with access controls
- Encrypt sensitive customer information (payment details, addresses)
- Regularly update customer records for accuracy

**Protection:**
- Do not share customer lists or contact details with third parties without consent
- Protect customer payment and financial information
- Respond promptly to customer data access or deletion requests
- Maintain customer privacy during delivery and service interactions

### **6.3 Employee Data Protection**

**Collection:**
- Collect employee data only for HR, payroll, compliance, and administrative purposes
- Inform employees about data collection and usage during onboarding

**Usage:**
- Use employee data only for legitimate employment-related purposes
- Maintain strict confidentiality of salary and compensation information
- Do not disclose employee personal information to unauthorized persons

**Storage:**
- Maintain employee records in secure HR filing systems
- Restrict access to HR data to authorized HR personnel and management only
- Store sensitive employee documents (salary, medical, personal) separately with enhanced security

**Protection:**
- Protect employee Aadhaar, PAN, bank details, and other sensitive information
- Do not share employee contact details for unauthorized purposes
- Maintain confidentiality of performance reviews and disciplinary records
- Provide employees access to their own data upon request

## **7. DATA RETENTION AND DISPOSAL**

### **7.1 Retention Periods**
Data shall be retained as per the following guidelines:

| **Type of Data** | **Retention Period** | **Legal Basis** |
|------------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Farmer procurement records | 7 years from last transaction | Income Tax Act, 1961 |
| Customer transaction records | 7 years from transaction date | Income Tax Act, 1961 |
| Employee records (current) | During employment + 7 years | Various labor laws |
| Financial records | 7 years from financial year end | Companies Act, 2013 |
| Tax documents (PF, ESI, TDS) | 7 years from relevant year | Respective tax laws |
| Contracts and agreements | 7 years after contract expiry | Contract Act, 1872 |
| Quality control records | 3-5 years | FSSAI regulations |
| Vehicle and logistics records | 3 years | Transport regulations |
| Temporary/operational data | Until purpose fulfilled | Business requirement |

### **7.2 Data Disposal**
When data reaches end of retention period:

**Physical Documents:**
- Shred documents using cross-cut shredder
- For highly sensitive documents, use professional destruction services with certificate of destruction
- Do not dispose confidential documents in regular trash
- Maintain disposal log for sensitive documents

**Digital Data:**
- Permanently delete files from systems (empty recycle bin)
- Overwrite deleted data using secure deletion tools
- Format storage devices before disposal or reuse
- Physically destroy hard drives/storage media containing highly sensitive data
- Maintain records of data deletion activities

## **8. DATA BREACH MANAGEMENT**

### **8.1 What is a Data Breach?**
A data breach is any incident where confidential or personal data is:
- Accessed by unauthorized persons
- Disclosed to unauthorized parties
- Lost, stolen, or misplaced
- Damaged or destroyed (including accidental deletion)
- Modified or corrupted without authorization

### **8.2 Reporting Data Breaches**
Any employee who discovers or suspects a data breach must:

1. **Immediate Action:**
   - Stop the breach if possible (e.g., secure the device, disconnect from network)
   - Do not attempt to cover up or ignore the breach
   - Immediately inform immediate supervisor and HR department
   - Document the incident (what happened, when, what data was affected)

2. **Reporting Timeline:**
   - Report within **1 hour** of discovering a potential breach
   - Provide all available details about the incident
   - Cooperate fully with investigation

3. **Investigation:**
   - Management will investigate the breach promptly
   - Affected parties (farmers, customers, employees) will be notified if required
   - Corrective actions will be implemented to prevent recurrence
   - Disciplinary action may be taken if breach resulted from negligence or misconduct

### **8.3 Consequences of Data Breach**
Depending on severity and intent, consequences may include:
- Verbal or written warning
- Suspension without pay
- Termination of employment
- Legal action and prosecution
- Recovery of damages caused by the breach
- Criminal liability under applicable laws

## **9. THIRD-PARTY DATA SHARING**

### **9.1 Authorized Sharing**
Confidential Information may be shared with third parties only when:
- Required for legitimate business purposes (e.g., logistics partners, payment processors)
- Authorized in writing by management
- Third party has signed confidentiality/non-disclosure agreement
- Minimum necessary information is shared
- Third party complies with data protection requirements

### **9.2 Prohibited Sharing**
The following sharing is strictly prohibited:
- Sharing with competitors or potential competitors
- Sharing with media or press without authorization
- Sharing with family, friends, or personal contacts
- Selling or commercializing company data
- Sharing on public forums, social media, or websites

### **9.3 Vendor Management**
When working with vendors/contractors who access company data:
- Ensure vendor has signed Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- Share only minimum required information
- Monitor vendor's data handling practices
- Terminate vendor access immediately upon contract completion
- Ensure vendor deletes/returns all data after service completion

## **11. COMPLIANCE AND LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This policy is designed to ensure compliance with applicable Indian laws including:

1. **Information Technology Act, 2000** and IT Rules, 2011
2. **Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011**
3. **Companies Act, 2013** (Section 2(85) - for sensitive data)
4. **Aadhaar (Targeted Delivery of Financial and Other Subsidies, Benefits and Services) Act, 2016**
5. **Payment of Wages Act, 1936**
6. **Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952**
7. **Income Tax Act, 1961** (for financial data retention)
8. **Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006** (for quality and traceability data)
9. **Contract Act, 1872**
10. **Indian Penal Code, 1860** (Sections related to breach of trust and confidentiality)
11. **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023** (once implemented)
