## **POSITION SUMMARY**

The General Administrative Assistant (Agri-Logistics & Farmer Coordination) is a multifaceted operational role responsible for bridging the gap between backward linkages (farmer aggregation and procurement) and forward linkages (marketing and customer delivery). This position requires a hands-on individual capable of coordinating with farmers, managing end-to-end logistics operations, driving heavy vehicles, and maintaining comprehensive records of all transactions. The role is critical to ensuring smooth supply chain operations from farm to customer while maintaining quality standards and operational efficiency.

---

## **KEY RESPONSIBILITIES**

### **A. BACKWARD LINKAGE OPERATIONS (40% of time)**

#### **1. Farmer Coordination & Relationship Management**
- Establish and maintain strong relationships with farmers and farmer producer organizations (FPOs)
- Act as the primary point of contact between the company and farmer networks
- Conduct regular visits to farmer locations to understand crop availability, harvest schedules, and challenges
- Communicate demand forecasts and procurement requirements to farmers in advance
- Educate farmers about company quality standards, grading parameters, and packaging requirements
- Resolve farmer grievances and payment-related queries promptly
- Organize farmer meetings and awareness programs as directed by management
- Onboard new farmers into the supply network following company protocols
- Maintain updated database of farmer contacts, locations, and crop profiles

#### **2. Procurement & Aggregation**
- Coordinate procurement of agricultural produce based on demand generated by the marketing team
- Visit farms/collection centers to inspect and verify quality of produce before procurement
- Ensure compliance with quality parameters including size, color, ripeness, moisture content, and damage levels
- Negotiate procurement prices within authorized limits and company guidelines
- Arrange weighing, grading, and sorting of produce at procurement points
- Issue gate passes and procurement receipts to farmers
- Monitor and report on crop availability, market prices, and procurement trends
- Identify alternate sourcing options during supply shortages
- Coordinate with Quality Control team for sample testing when required

#### **3. Quality Assurance at Source**
- Conduct visual inspection of produce at farm/collection centers
- Ensure adherence to food safety and hygiene standards during handling
- Reject substandard produce and provide feedback to farmers for improvement
- Document quality parameters for each procurement batch
- Report quality issues or contamination risks immediately to supervisors
- Implement corrective actions to prevent quality deterioration during transit

---

### **B. LOGISTICS & SUPPLY CHAIN MANAGEMENT (35% of time)**

#### **4. Transportation Management - Farm to Storage**
- Plan and execute transportation of procured produce from farmer locations to company storage facilities
- Drive heavy vehicles (trucks/tempos) loaded with agricultural produce safely and efficiently
- Ensure proper loading techniques to minimize damage, bruising, and wastage
- Coordinate with loading staff and farmers for timely pickup
- Monitor cold chain requirements for perishable items
- Plan optimal routes considering distance, road conditions, and delivery timelines
- Ensure vehicle is properly cleaned and sanitized before loading fresh produce
- Conduct pre-trip vehicle inspections for safety and roadworthiness
- Maintain driving logs, fuel records, and vehicle maintenance schedules
- Report any vehicle breakdowns or delays immediately to management

#### **5. Transportation Management - Storage to Customer**
- Coordinate delivery of packed products from storage facility to customers/distribution points
- Plan delivery schedules based on customer orders and urgency
- Drive vehicles for customer deliveries when required
- Ensure timely delivery within committed timeframes
- Obtain delivery acknowledgments and proof of delivery (POD) from customers
- Handle customer queries related to delivery timings and product condition
- Coordinate with third-party transporters when required
- Track consignments and update delivery status in real-time

#### **6. Loading & Unloading Operations**
- Supervise and participate in loading operations at procurement points
- Ensure proper stacking, arrangement, and securing of goods in vehicles
- Use appropriate handling equipment (trolleys, pallet jacks, hand trucks) safely
- Supervise unloading at storage facilities ensuring careful handling
- Verify quantities during loading and unloading against dispatch documents
- Report any damages, shortages, or discrepancies immediately
- Ensure cleanliness and hygiene during all handling operations
- Follow manual handling safety guidelines to prevent injuries

#### **7. Storage Facility Coordination**
- Coordinate with storage facility managers for receipt of incoming goods
- Ensure proper handover of goods with necessary documentation
- Monitor storage conditions (temperature, humidity, ventilation) for quality preservation
- Coordinate dispatch of goods from storage to customers as per orders
- Maintain first-in-first-out (FIFO) principles for inventory rotation
- Report storage capacity constraints or facility issues to management

---

### **C. PACKAGING, BRANDING & PRODUCT PREPARATION (10% of time)**

#### **8. Packaging Operations**
- Supervise packaging operations as per company standards and customer specifications
- Ensure proper cleaning, grading, and sorting before packaging
- Monitor packaging quality including seal integrity, labeling accuracy, and presentation
- Coordinate with packaging vendors for timely supply of materials
- Maintain inventory of packaging materials (boxes, crates, bags, labels, tapes)
- Place orders for packaging supplies as needed
- Ensure packaging complies with food safety regulations and export standards (if applicable)
- Implement corrective actions for packaging defects or customer complaints

#### **9. Branding & Labeling**
- Ensure all products are correctly labeled with brand name, product details, and batch information
- Verify accuracy of labels including weight, MRP, manufacturing date, and best before date
- Ensure regulatory compliance in labeling (FSSAI license number, nutritional information, etc.)
- Coordinate with marketing team for promotional packaging or special labeling requirements
- Maintain consistency in branding across all product batches
- Report any labeling errors or branding inconsistencies immediately

---

### **D. DOCUMENTATION & RECORD KEEPING (10% of time)**

#### **10. Transaction Records & Data Management**
- Maintain accurate and up-to-date records of all procurement transactions
- Record farmer details, procurement date, quantity, quality grade, and price paid
- Document vehicle movement logs, fuel consumption, and maintenance records
- Maintain stock registers showing inward and outward movement of goods
- Prepare and maintain dispatch documents, delivery challans, and invoices
- Record customer delivery details including delivery time, recipient name, and feedback
- Update daily progress reports on procurement, logistics, and deliveries
- Maintain physical and digital records as per company format
- Ensure all documents are properly filed and easily retrievable

#### **11. Quality & Compliance Documentation**
- Document quality inspection results for each procurement batch
- Maintain quality control checklists and inspection reports
- Record rejections, damages, and wastage with reasons
- Keep records of cold chain temperature logs for perishable items
- Maintain certificates of analysis, phytosanitary certificates (if applicable)
- Document customer complaints and resolution actions
- Maintain food safety and hygiene compliance records

#### **12. Financial Records & Reconciliation**
- Maintain records of payments made to farmers
- Track advances given and payments pending
- Record transportation costs, fuel expenses, and other logistics costs
- Submit expense reports with supporting bills and receipts
- Reconcile physical stock with documented records regularly
- Report any discrepancies in payments, quantities, or pricing
- Assist in monthly/quarterly financial reconciliation as required

#### **13. Data Entry & System Updates**
- Enter procurement, sales, and logistics data into company software/ERP system
- Update farmer database with latest contact information and transaction history
- Update inventory levels in real-time
- Generate reports from system as required by management
- Ensure data accuracy and completeness
- Backup critical data as per company protocol

---

### **E. COORDINATION & COMMUNICATION (5% of time)**

#### **14. Inter-Department Coordination**
- Act as liaison between backward linkage (procurement) and forward linkage (marketing/sales) teams
- Communicate procurement status and product availability to marketing team daily
- Receive and understand demand forecasts and customer orders from sales team
- Coordinate with finance team for farmer payments and expense approvals
- Work with quality control team for product testing and certification
- Collaborate with warehouse team for storage and dispatch operations
- Provide feedback to management on operational challenges and improvements

#### **15. Customer & Stakeholder Communication**
- Communicate delivery schedules to customers proactively
- Handle customer queries related to product availability and delivery timelines
- Maintain professional and courteous communication with all stakeholders
- Escalate critical issues to supervisors promptly
- Participate in team meetings and provide operational updates
- Share market intelligence and competitor information with management
