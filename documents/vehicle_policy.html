VEHICLE USAGE POLICY

1. POLICY OVERVIEW

1.1 Purpose

This Vehicle Usage Policy establishes guidelines and procedures for the use, operation, and maintenance of company-owned or company-assigned vehicles by employees of Kisaan Infotech Limited. This policy aims to:

  • Ensure safe and responsible use of company vehicles
  • Protect company assets and minimize liability
  • Define employee responsibilities regarding vehicle usage
  • Establish procedures for vehicle maintenance and accident reporting
  • Promote fuel efficiency and cost optimization
  • Ensure compliance with traffic laws and regulations

1.2 Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All employees authorized to drive company vehicles
  • All company-owned vehicles including trucks, tempos, cars, and two-wheelers
  • Leased or rented vehicles used for company business
  • Personal vehicles used for company business (where applicable)

1.3 Effective Date

This policy is effective from the date of vehicle assignment to the employee.

2. ELIGIBILITY AND AUTHORIZATION

2.1 Driver Eligibility

To be eligible to drive a company vehicle, an employee must:

  • Possess a valid driving license appropriate for the vehicle category (LMV/HMV)
  • Have the driving license endorsed for the specific vehicle type being operated
  • Be at least 21 years of age (18 years for two-wheelers)
  • Have minimum driving experience as specified in the job description
  • Pass company driving assessment test (if applicable)
  • Have a clean driving record with no major violations in the past 3 years
  • Complete company vehicle safety training
  • Sign this Vehicle Usage Policy acknowledgment

2.2 License Requirements

2.3 License Verification

  • Employees must provide a copy of valid driving license before vehicle assignment
  • License must be verified by HR department and kept on file
  • Employees must immediately inform HR of any license suspension, revocation, or expiry
  • Driving with expired or invalid license will result in immediate disciplinary action
  • License renewal is the employee's responsibility

2.4 Vehicle Assignment

  • Vehicles will be assigned based on job requirements and availability
  • Assignment will be documented with vehicle handover form
  • Employee will be provided with vehicle registration papers, insurance copy, and PUC certificate
  • Keys, documents, and accessories must be handed over at the time of assignment
  • Vehicle reassignment or withdrawal can be done at company's discretion

3. AUTHORIZED USE

3.1 Business Use Only

Company vehicles are provided strictly for official business purposes. Authorized uses include:

  • Travel for procurement activities (farm visits, collection centers)
  • Transportation of agricultural produce from farms to storage/customers
  • Customer deliveries and service visits
  • Meetings and business appointments
  • Training and official events
  • Emergency company business

3.2 Prohibited Uses

Company vehicles SHALL NOT be used for:

  • Personal errands or non-business activities
  • Commuting between home and office (unless specifically authorized)
  • Transporting non-employees without authorization
  • Carrying personal goods or materials
  • Commercial purposes unrelated to company business
  • Any illegal activities or purposes
  • Towing other vehicles (unless company-authorized)
  • Off-road driving or racing
  • Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Transportation of hazardous or prohibited materials (except authorized agricultural products)

3.3 Personal Use (If Authorized)

  • Personal use of company vehicle is generally not permitted
  • In exceptional cases where authorized in writing:
    • Personal use is limited to specified occasions only
    • Fuel for personal use must be paid by employee
    • All other policy terms remain applicable
    • Insurance coverage may be limited during personal use
    • Company reserves right to withdraw authorization anytime

4. DRIVER RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 General Responsibilities

Employees authorized to drive company vehicles must:

Before Driving:

  • Conduct pre-trip vehicle inspection (tires, lights, oil, water, brakes)
  • Ensure vehicle is in roadworthy condition
  • Check that all required documents are in the vehicle (registration, insurance, PUC)
  • Ensure all safety equipment is present (first aid kit, fire extinguisher, reflective triangle)
  • Adjust mirrors and seat for safe driving
  • Ensure proper loading and securing of cargo

While Driving:

  • Obey all traffic rules, regulations, and road signs
  • Drive within speed limits and according to road conditions
  • Use seat belts at all times
  • Avoid aggressive or reckless driving
  • Do not use mobile phones while driving (use hands-free if necessary)
  • Do not drive when tired, ill, or under stress
  • Take regular breaks on long journeys (15 minutes every 2 hours)
  • Be courteous to other road users
  • Report any vehicle defects or unusual sounds immediately

After Driving:

  • Park vehicle in designated secure parking area
  • Lock all doors and windows
  • Remove keys and documents from vehicle
  • Report any incidents, damages, or issues immediately
  • Complete trip log and fuel records

4.2 Vehicle Cleanliness

  • Keep vehicle interior and exterior clean at all times
  • Remove trash and debris after each trip
  • Ensure produce residues are cleaned after transportation
  • Sanitize vehicle regularly, especially food contact surfaces
  • Company will arrange periodic professional cleaning
  • Employee is responsible for day-to-day cleanliness

4.3 Security

  • Never leave vehicle unattended with engine running
  • Always lock vehicle when parked
  • Do not leave valuables visible in the vehicle
  • Park in well-lit, secure areas when possible
  • Never leave keys in ignition when vehicle is unattended
  • Report theft or break-in immediately to police and company
  • Do not lend vehicle keys to unauthorized persons

5.2 Maintenance Procedures

  • Employee must maintain vehicle logbook recording all trips and maintenance
  • Report any maintenance needs or defects immediately
  • Do not ignore warning lights or unusual vehicle behavior
  • Schedule maintenance during non-peak hours when possible
  • Use only company-authorized service centers/mechanics
  • Obtain prior approval for any non-emergency repairs exceeding ₹500/-
  • Submit maintenance bills/receipts to accounts department within 3 days
  • Keep vehicle off-road during scheduled maintenance

5.3 Daily Checks

Employee must check daily:

  • Tire pressure and condition
  • Engine oil level
  • Coolant/water level
  • Brake fluid level
  • Battery condition
  • Lights and indicators functioning
  • Windshield wipers and washer fluid
  • Horn and mirrors
  • Any visible damage or leaks

5.4 Breakdown Procedures

In case of vehicle breakdown:

  1. Move vehicle to safe location if possible
  2. Turn on hazard lights and place warning triangle
  3. Call company emergency number immediately
  4. Do not attempt major repairs yourself
  5. Wait for authorized mechanic or tow service
  6. Complete breakdown report form
  7. Inform supervisor about delay in operations

6. FUEL MANAGEMENT

6.1 Fuel Policy

  • Company will provide fuel for all authorized business use
  • Fuel cards or petrol vouchers will be issued where applicable
  • Employee must maintain accurate fuel records in vehicle logbook
  • Record odometer reading, liters filled, amount, and location for each refueling

6.2 Fuel Efficiency

Employees are expected to:

  • Practice fuel-efficient driving (smooth acceleration, maintaining steady speed)
  • Avoid unnecessary idling
  • Plan routes to minimize distance and fuel consumption
  • Keep tires properly inflated
  • Remove unnecessary weight from vehicle
  • Use air conditioning judiciously
  • Report unusual fuel consumption immediately

6.3 Fuel Records

  • Maintain fuel log with date, odometer reading, quantity, amount, and vendor
  • Submit fuel bills/receipts to accounts within 3 days
  • Company will monitor fuel consumption and efficiency
  • Unexplained fuel consumption may require investigation
  • Fuel theft or misuse will result in disciplinary action

7. ACCIDENT AND INCIDENT REPORTING

7.1 Immediate Actions at Accident Scene

In case of accident, the driver must:

Priority 1 - Safety:

  1. Stop immediately at the scene (do not flee)
  2. Turn on hazard lights
  3. Check for injuries to self and others
  4. Call emergency services (108/102) if anyone is injured
  5. Move to safe location if vehicle is blocking traffic
  6. Set up warning triangle

Priority 2 - Documentation:

  1. Call police (100) if there is injury, death, or significant property damage
  2. Take photographs of accident scene, damage, and vehicle positions
  3. Note details of other vehicle(s): registration number, driver name, contact, insurance
  4. Collect witness information (names, contact numbers)
  5. Do NOT sign any document admitting fault
  6. Do NOT make any payment at the scene
  7. Exchange insurance information with other party

Priority 3 - Company Notification:

  1. Inform immediate supervisor immediately (within 1 hour)
  2. Inform company insurance department
  3. Inform HR department
  4. Do not speak to media or make public statements

7.2 Accident Reporting Requirements

  • Submit written accident report within 24 hours using company accident report form
  • Include all details: date, time, location, circumstances, damages, witnesses
  • Attach copies of police report, FIR, photographs, and other relevant documents
  • Cooperate fully with police investigation
  • Attend police station hearings as required
  • Inform company of any legal notices received

7.3 Insurance Claims

  • Company will initiate insurance claim process
  • Employee must cooperate fully with insurance company investigation
  • Provide all requested documents and information promptly
  • Attend survey and damage assessment appointments
  • Do not get vehicle repaired before insurance survey (except in emergency)
  • Keep damaged vehicle in secure location until claim settlement

7.4 Minor Incidents

For minor incidents (scratches, dents, no third-party involvement):

  • Report to supervisor within 24 hours
  • Complete incident report form
  • Submit photographs of damage
  • Company will decide repair action
  • Repeated minor incidents may indicate careless driving

8. TRAFFIC VIOLATIONS AND PENALTIES

8.1 Traffic Rules Compliance

  • All drivers must strictly comply with Motor Vehicles Act and traffic rules
  • Common violations to avoid:
    • Over-speeding
    • Jumping red lights
    • Wrong-way driving
    • Driving without seat belt
    • Using mobile phone while driving
    • Overloading vehicle
    • Driving without valid documents
    • Parking violations
    • Drunk driving (zero tolerance)

8.2 Liability for Violations

Employee Fault: If violation occurs during business use due to driver negligence:

  • Employee is personally responsible for fines and penalties
  • Amount will be deducted from salary
  • Repeated violations will result in disciplinary action
  • License suspension due to violations may lead to termination

Company Responsibility: Company will bear costs if violation is due to:

  • Vehicle defects despite proper maintenance
  • Following legitimate company instructions
  • Circumstances beyond driver's control

8.3 Serious Violations

The following violations will result in immediate disciplinary action:

  • Driving under influence of alcohol or drugs (immediate termination)
  • Hit and run (immediate termination)
  • Reckless or dangerous driving
  • Driving without valid license
  • Causing accident due to gross negligence
  • Using vehicle for illegal activities

9. LOADING AND CARGO GUIDELINES

9.1 Loading Procedures

  • Do not exceed vehicle's rated load capacity
  • Ensure even distribution of weight
  • Secure all cargo properly using ropes, straps, or nets
  • Do not block driver's view with cargo
  • Ensure cargo does not extend beyond vehicle dimensions (or follow regulations if it does)
  • Cover loose cargo to prevent spillage
  • Load perishable items in properly ventilated or refrigerated compartments

9.2 Special Considerations for Agricultural Produce

  • Use clean crates/containers for fruits and vegetables
  • Avoid stacking beyond recommended height
  • Ensure proper ventilation to prevent spoilage
  • Handle produce gently to avoid bruising
  • Follow cold chain requirements for temperature-sensitive items
  • Clean vehicle after unloading to prevent contamination
  • Do not mix different produce types without proper separation

9.3 Prohibited Cargo

Do not transport:

  • Hazardous materials (explosives, flammable liquids, chemicals) without authorization
  • Illegal goods or contraband
  • Live animals (except as specifically authorized)
  • Unauthorized passengers
  • Personal goods along with company cargo

10. PASSENGER POLICY

10.1 Authorized Passengers

Company vehicles may carry:

  • Company employees on official business
  • Farmers during procurement activities (with prior approval)
  • Customers or business partners (with approval)
  • Service personnel or contractors (with approval)

10.2 Passenger Safety

  • All passengers must wear seat belts
  • Do not exceed vehicle seating capacity
  • Children under 12 should not sit in front seat
  • Ensure passengers do not distract driver
  • Driver is responsible for passenger safety

10.3 Unauthorized Passengers

Do not carry:

  • Family members or friends on business trips
  • Hitchhikers
  • Passengers for personal gain
  • Unauthorized persons during produce transportation

11. PARKING AND OVERNIGHT STORAGE

11.1 Company Premises Parking

  • Park vehicle in designated company parking area when not in use
  • Follow parking guidelines and markings
  • Do not block other vehicles or entry/exit points
  • Ensure vehicle is locked and secured

11.2 Outside Parking

When parking outside company premises:

  • Use secure, well-lit parking areas
  • Avoid parking in no-parking zones
  • Pay for parking where required (company will reimburse genuine parking fees)
  • Do not leave valuables visible in vehicle
  • Note parking location and submit in trip report

11.3 Overnight Parking

  • For overnight trips, park vehicle in secure hotel/facility parking
  • Inform supervisor of vehicle location
  • Ensure vehicle is properly locked
  • Keep keys and documents safe

12. VEHICLE RETURN AND HANDOVER

12.1 Temporary Return

When returning vehicle temporarily (end of day, weekend):

  • Park in designated area
  • Ensure vehicle is clean
  • Remove all personal items
  • Hand over keys to designated person
  • Report any damage or issues
  • Submit trip log and fuel records

12.2 Permanent Handover

Upon termination, transfer, or end of vehicle assignment:

  • Return vehicle in good condition (normal wear and tear excepted)
  • Vehicle should be clean inside and outside
  • Return all keys, documents, accessories, and equipment
  • Clear all dues (fines, damages, fuel advances)
  • Complete vehicle handover checklist
  • Any damage beyond normal wear will be charged to employee
  • Final settlement subject to vehicle condition verification

13.2 Emergency Situations

Vehicle Fire:

  • Stop vehicle immediately in safe location
  • Turn off ignition
  • Evacuate all passengers
  • Use fire extinguisher if safe to do so
  • Call fire brigade (101)
  • Move away from vehicle
  • Call company immediately

Medical Emergency:

  • Call ambulance (108/102) immediately
  • Provide first aid if trained
  • Do not move seriously injured person unless in danger
  • Note location for ambulance
  • Inform company and patient's family
  • Accompany to hospital if needed

Vehicle Theft:

  • Report to nearest police station immediately
  • File FIR and obtain copy
  • Inform company within 1 hour
  • Inform insurance company
  • Provide all vehicle and key details
  • Cooperate with police investigation

14. CONSEQUENCES OF POLICY VIOLATION

14.1 Minor Violations

Examples: Late reporting, poor cleanliness, minor documentation errors

  • First Offense: Verbal warning and counseling
  • Second Offense: Written warning
  • Third Offense: Suspension of driving privileges for 15-30 days

14.2 Moderate Violations

Examples: Unauthorized use, failure to report accident, traffic violations, poor maintenance

  • First Offense: Written warning and possible suspension
  • Second Offense: Suspension without pay (3-7 days)
  • Third Offense: Permanent withdrawal of driving privileges

14.3 Serious Violations

Examples: Reckless driving, causing accident due to negligence, vehicle misuse

  • First Offense: Suspension without pay (7-15 days) + damages recovery
  • Second Offense: Final written warning + extended suspension
  • Third Offense: Termination of employment

14.4 Gross Violations

Examples: Drunk driving, hit and run, using vehicle for illegal activities, theft

  • Action: Immediate termination of employment + legal action + recovery of damages

14.5 Financial Liability

Employee may be held financially liable for:

  • Damages caused due to negligence or policy violation
  • Traffic fines and penalties due to employee fault
  • Loss or damage to company vehicle or cargo
  • Cost of repairs for damage beyond normal wear and tear
  • Insurance deductible if accident was preventable
  • Cost of replacement keys or documents if lost
