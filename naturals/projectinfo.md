### **4.0 Manufacturing Process**

The core process remains similar but is customized for each raw material:

1.  **Sourcing:** Procurement from certified organic partner farms.
2.  **Primary Processing:**
    *   **Leaves (Papaya, Moringa):** Washing, shade-drying.
    *   **Roots (Ashwagandha, Shatavari):** Washing, peeling (if required), cutting, drying.
    *   **Stems (Giloy):** Washing, cutting into slices, drying.
    *   **Rhizomes (Ginger):** Washing, peeling, slicing, drying.
3.  **Drying:** Using a combination of large-scale shaded areas and a **mechanical dryer** (crucial for consistency and handling larger volumes, especially for moisture-rich roots like Ginger and Ashwagandha).
4.  **Grinding/Pulverizing:** Using a high-capacity commercial pulverizer with different mesh sizes for fine powder.
5.  **Packaging:** Product-specific packaging. Powders in UV-protected jars/pouches; dried slices in transparent, food-grade pouches to showcase quality.
6.  **Quality Check & Storage:** Final QC check before storage in a cool, dry warehouse.

### **7.0 Break-Even Analysis**

**1. Contribution Margin:**
*   Total Monthly Revenue = ₹ 612,500
*   Total Monthly Variable Cost (TVC) = ₹ 306,250
*   **Total Contribution Margin (TCM)** = Revenue - TVC = ₹ 612,500 - ₹ 306,250 = **₹ 306,250**
*   **Contribution Margin Ratio (CMR)** = TCM / Revenue = ₹ 306,250 / ₹ 612,500 = **0.50 or 50%**

**2. Break-Even Point in Revenue (Sales):**
*   **BEP (Revenue)** = Total Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin Ratio
*   BEP (Revenue) = ₹ 170,000 / 0.50 = **₹ 340,000 per month.**

**Interpretation:**
Prakriti Vault Naturals must generate **₹ 340,000 in monthly sales revenue** to cover all its fixed and variable costs. This means the business needs to operate at about **55.5%** (₹340,000 / ₹612,500) of its target capacity to break even.

**Break-Even Timeline:**
*   Assuming a 6-month ramp-up period to reach full sales capacity, the cumulative revenue will cross the cumulative total cost around the **14th month**, marking the overall business break-even point.

### **9.0 Risk Analysis & Mitigation**

*   **Risk:** Complex supply chain for multiple raw materials.
    *   **Mitigation:** Develop strong, long-term relationships with 2-3 suppliers for each key herb.
*   **Risk:** Cross-contamination during processing.
    *   **Mitigation:** Implement a strict cleaning schedule for machinery between batches. Process one product type at a time.
*   **Risk:** Higher initial investment and working capital requirement.
    *   **Mitigation:** Phased implementation; start with 3-4 products and expand. Pre-sell products to generate initial cash flow.
*   **Risk:** Varying shelf-life of different products.
    *   **Mitigation:** Implement a robust First-Expiry-First-Out (FEFO) inventory management system.

### **Implementation Advice: A Phased Approach**

Do not launch all 13 products at once. A phased approach manages cash flow and operational complexity.

*   **Phase 1 (Months 1-6):** Launch with the **Core 5**: Moringa, Ashwagandha, Shatavari, Turmeric, and one dried product (e.g., Ginger). This establishes your brand with high-demand, high-margin staples.
*   **Phase 2 (Months 7-12):** Introduce **Secondary 4**: Brahmi, Amla, Tulsi, and Giloy. This expands your wellness range and provides ingredients for future blends.
*   **Phase 3 (Year 2):** Launch **Value-Added 4**: Triphala Powder, Fenugreek Powder, and 2-3 Herbal Tea Blends. This is where you maximize profitability and customer loyalty with formulated products.

### **Financial & Operational Impact of Expansion:**

*   **Increased Investment:** You will need a slightly higher initial investment for a larger dryer, more storage space, and diverse raw material inventory. An increase of **₹ 3-5 Lakhs** (~$3,600-$6,000) would be prudent.
*   **Higher Breakeven Point:** With more fixed costs (storage, marketing), your monthly breakeven revenue will increase, perhaps to around **₹ 400,000 - ₹ 450,000**. However, the expanded portfolio makes this *easier* to achieve because you have more products to sell.
*   **Major Benefit - Risk Diversification:** If one product (e.g., Papaya Leaf) sees a demand drop, your business is sustained by Moringa, Ashwagandha, Teas, etc. You are no longer reliant on one "hero" product.

**Final Recommendation:** Start with the phased approach. **Triphala** and **Herbal Tea Blends** should be your primary targets for expansion after the first year, as they represent the highest margin and most strategic growth for your business. Including **Brahmi** and **Tulsi** early on is also strongly advised due to their high demand and synergy with your existing products.
