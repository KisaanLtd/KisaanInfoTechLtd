KISAAN INFOTECH LIMITED

CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION POLICY

1. POLICY OVERVIEW
1.1 Purpose
This Confidentiality and Data Protection Policy establishes guidelines for handling, protecting, and maintaining the confidentiality of sensitive information belonging to Kisaan Infotech Limited ("the Company"), its customers, farmers, business partners, and employees. This policy aims to:
  • Protect the Company's proprietary and confidential information
  • Ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws in India
  • Maintain trust and integrity in business relationships
  • Prevent unauthorized disclosure, misuse, or loss of confidential data
  • Define employee responsibilities regarding information security
1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
  • All employees (permanent, probationary, contractual, and temporary)
  • Consultants, contractors, and third-party service providers
  • Interns, trainees, and apprentices
  • All forms of information (physical, electronic, verbal, and visual)
  • Information accessed, created, stored, or transmitted during employment
1.3 Effective Date
This policy is effective from the date of employee's joining and continues to remain in force even after termination of employment.
1.4 Policy Owner
This policy is owned and maintained by the Management of Kisaan Infotech Limited. Any questions or clarifications should be directed to the HR Department or immediate supervisor.
2. DEFINITIONS
2.1 Confidential Information
Confidential Information includes, but is not limited to:
A. Business Information
  • Business strategies, plans, and forecasts
  • Marketing and sales strategies
  • Pricing information, cost structures, and profit margins
  • Financial data, budgets, and projections
  • Procurement and sourcing strategies
  • Vendor and supplier information
  • Business proposals and contracts
  • Market research and competitive intelligence
  • Operational procedures and processes
  • Trade secrets and proprietary methodologies
B. Farmer & Customer Data
  • Farmer personal details (names, addresses, contact numbers, bank details, Aadhaar, PAN)
  • Farmer production data, crop patterns, and landholding information
  • Procurement prices, payment terms, and transaction history
  • Customer details (names, addresses, contact information)
  • Customer orders, purchase history, and preferences
  • Delivery addresses and schedules
  • Credit terms and payment information
  • Customer complaints and feedback
C. Employee Information
  • Personal employee data (addresses, contact details, family information)
  • Salary information and compensation details
  • Performance reviews and appraisal records
  • Disciplinary actions and grievances
  • Medical records and health information
  • Bank account details and tax information
2.2 Personal Data
Personal Data means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including biographical data, contact information, identification numbers, financial information, biometric data, photographic images, and any other information that can identify an individual.
2.3 Sensitive Personal Data
Sensitive Personal Data includes:
  • Passwords and financial information (bank account, credit card, debit card)
  • Physical, physiological, and mental health conditions
  • Medical records and history
  • Biometric information
  • Sexual orientation
3. CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS
3.1 General Obligations
All employees must:
  1. Maintain Confidentiality: Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential during and after employment
  2. Use Only for Business Purposes: Use Confidential Information solely for performing authorized job duties
  3. Need-to-Know Basis: Access only the information necessary for job responsibilities
  4. Prevent Unauthorized Disclosure: Take all reasonable measures to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure
  5. Secure Storage: Store documents and data securely when not in use
  6. Report Breaches: Immediately report any suspected or actual breach of confidentiality
  7. Return Materials: Return all confidential materials upon termination or when no longer needed
3.2 Prohibited Actions
Employees must NOT:
  1. Disclose Confidential Information to unauthorized persons, including family members and friends
  2. Discuss confidential matters in public places
  3. Remove confidential documents from office premises without authorization
  4. Copy, photograph, or reproduce confidential materials without permission
  5. Use Confidential Information for personal benefit or gain
  6. Use Confidential Information to compete with the Company
  7. Disclose Confidential Information to competitors, media, or any third parties
  8. Post or share confidential information on social media, forums, or public platforms
  9. Access information beyond their authorized level or job requirements
  10. Share passwords, login credentials, or access codes with others
4. DATA PROTECTION PRINCIPLES
The Company and its employees shall adhere to the following data protection principles:
4.1 Lawfulness and Fairness
  • Data shall be collected and processed lawfully and fairly
  • Purpose of data collection shall be clearly communicated
  • Consent shall be obtained where required by law
4.2 Purpose Limitation
  • Data shall be collected only for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes
  • Data shall not be used for purposes incompatible with the original purpose
4.3 Data Minimization
  • Only necessary and relevant data shall be collected
  • Excessive or irrelevant data collection shall be avoided
4.4 Accuracy
  • Data shall be accurate, complete, and kept up to date
  • Reasonable steps shall be taken to correct or delete inaccurate data
4.5 Storage Limitation
  • Data shall be retained only for as long as necessary for the stated purpose
  • Data shall be securely deleted or destroyed when no longer required
4.6 Integrity and Confidentiality
  • Appropriate security measures shall be implemented to protect data
  • Data shall be protected against unauthorized access, loss, damage, or destruction
5. DATA SECURITY MEASURES
5.1 Physical Security
Documents and Records:
  • Store all physical documents containing confidential information in locked cabinets/drawers
  • Do not leave confidential documents unattended on desks
  • Shred or destroy confidential documents before disposal
  • Lock office premises when leaving
5.2 Digital Security
Computer and Device Security:
  • Use strong passwords (minimum 8 characters with alphanumeric and special characters)
  • Change passwords regularly (every 90 days minimum)
  • Do not share passwords or login credentials
  • Lock computer/device when leaving workstation
  • Install and update antivirus software regularly
  • Encrypt sensitive data stored on laptops or portable devices
Email Security:
  • Use company email only for business communication
  • Do not send confidential information to personal email accounts
  • Verify recipient email addresses before sending confidential information
  • Mark emails containing sensitive information as "Confidential"
  • Report phishing attempts to IT department immediately
5.3 Communication Security
  • Do not discuss confidential matters in public places
  • Use private meeting rooms for confidential discussions
  • Be mindful of phone conversations in open areas
  • Do not post or share any company information on social media platforms
6. SPECIFIC DATA PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS
6.1 Farmer Data Protection
Collection:
  • Collect farmer data only for legitimate business purposes
  • Obtain verbal or written consent from farmers for data collection
  • Inform farmers about how their data will be used
  • Collect only necessary information
Protection:
  • Do not share farmer contact details with competitors or unauthorized parties
  • Protect farmer bank account and payment information with highest security
  • Do not use farmer data for personal benefit or unauthorized purposes
  • Ensure farmers' Aadhaar and PAN information is stored securely
6.2 Customer Data Protection
  • Use customer data only for order processing, delivery, and customer service
  • Do not share customer lists or contact details with third parties without consent
  • Protect customer payment and financial information
  • Maintain customer privacy during delivery and service interactions
6.3 Employee Data Protection
  • Use employee data only for HR, payroll, compliance, and administrative purposes
  • Maintain strict confidentiality of salary and compensation information
  • Protect employee Aadhaar, PAN, bank details, and other sensitive information
  • Maintain confidentiality of performance reviews and disciplinary records
7. DATA RETENTION AND DISPOSAL
7.1 Retention Periods
Type of Data Retention Period Legal Basis
Farmer procurement records 7 years from last transaction Income Tax Act, 1961
Customer transaction records 7 years from transaction date Income Tax Act, 1961
Employee records (current) During employment + 7 years Various labor laws
Financial records 7 years from financial year end Companies Act, 2013
Tax documents (PF, ESI, TDS) 7 years from relevant year Respective tax laws
7.2 Data Disposal
Physical Documents:
  • Shred documents using cross-cut shredder
  • Do not dispose confidential documents in regular trash
  • Maintain disposal log for sensitive documents
Digital Data:
  • Permanently delete files from systems
  • Overwrite deleted data using secure deletion tools
  • Physically destroy hard drives containing highly sensitive data
8. DATA BREACH MANAGEMENT
8.1 What is a Data Breach?
A data breach is any incident where confidential or personal data is:
  • Accessed by unauthorized persons
  • Disclosed to unauthorized parties
  • Lost, stolen, or misplaced
  • Damaged or destroyed
  • Modified or corrupted without authorization
8.2 Reporting Data Breaches
Any employee who discovers or suspects a data breach must:
  1. Stop the breach if possible
  2. Do not attempt to cover up or ignore the breach
  3. Immediately inform immediate supervisor and HR department
  4. Document the incident
  5. Report within 1 hour of discovering a potential breach
  6. Cooperate fully with investigation
8.3 Consequences of Data Breach
Depending on severity and intent, consequences may include:
  • Verbal or written warning
  • Suspension without pay
  • Termination of employment
  • Legal action and prosecution
  • Recovery of damages caused by the breach
9. THIRD-PARTY DATA SHARING
9.1 Authorized Sharing
Confidential Information may be shared with third parties only when:
  • Required for legitimate business purposes
  • Authorized in writing by management
  • Third party has signed confidentiality/non-disclosure agreement
  • Minimum necessary information is shared
9.2 Prohibited Sharing
  • Sharing with competitors or potential competitors
  • Sharing with media or press without authorization
  • Sharing with family, friends, or personal contacts
  • Selling or commercializing company data
  • Sharing on public forums, social media, or websites
10. EMPLOYEE RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES
10.1 Employee Rights
  • Access their own personal data held by the Company
  • Request correction of inaccurate personal data
  • Understand how their personal data is being used
  • Report data breaches without fear of retaliation
10.2 Employee Responsibilities
  • Reading and understanding this policy
  • Attending data protection training programs
  • Following all security procedures and protocols
  • Protecting their own login credentials and access rights
  • Reporting security incidents or policy violations
  • Maintaining confidentiality even after leaving employment
11. COMPLIANCE AND LEGAL FRAMEWORK
This policy is designed to ensure compliance with applicable Indian laws including:
  1. Information Technology Act, 2000 and IT Rules, 2011
  2. Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules, 2011
  3. Companies Act, 2013
  4. Aadhaar Act, 2016
  5. Payment of Wages Act, 1936
  6. Employees' Provident Funds Act, 1952
  7. Income Tax Act, 1961
  8. Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006
  9. Indian Penal Code, 1860
  10. Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023
12. CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE
12.1 Disciplinary Action
  • First Offense: Written warning and mandatory retraining
  • Second Offense: Suspension without pay and final warning
  • Serious/Willful Violation: Immediate termination of employment
12.2 Legal Action
The Company reserves the right to:
  • Initiate civil proceedings for damages
  • File criminal complaints under applicable laws
  • Seek injunctions to prevent further breaches
  • Recover monetary losses caused by breach
13. TRAINING AND AWARENESS
  • All new employees must complete confidentiality and data protection training during induction
  • Annual refresher training on data protection
  • Training on new policies or regulatory changes
  • All employees must sign an acknowledgment form
